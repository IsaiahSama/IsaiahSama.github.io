About The Founder

My name is Isaiah Carrington, Founder of Isaiah's Innovative Solutions. I am currently 17 years old and a student in my preliminary year, doing a Double Major in Computer Science and Mathematics at the University of The West Indies Cavehill Campus. I am a self taught programmer specializing in Python. My goal is to become a Data Scientist and get into Machine Learning and AI Creation, and Isaiah's Innovative Solutions was developed to help me build the connections necessary and to begin working towards my goal.

The Origin of Isaiah's Innovative Solutions

The concept for Isaiah's Innovative Solutions came about when I was given the job of creating a presentation for a UWI student by my Robotics mentor using my recently developed skills in Scratch Programming on the 26th November 2020. I had thoughts of starting my own business in the programming field for about a year, but all I was missing was a push, actually doing it. I had completed the job on the 27th November 2020. On the 29th of November, 2020, I made up my mind that I would no longer just sit around thinking about starting a business, but that I would actually start pushing towards it. Originally, the name I had was Isaiah's Innovations, however, on talking it over with my partner, I decided on Isaiah's Innovative Solutions. About a week after the job, I received word that the presentation was warmly accepted and that my client's group had received a good grade for their project. This one job helped me to realize that I had the confidence needed and was finally ready to start putting my skills to use.

My Programming Journey

The Beginning

I've been interested in computers from about the time I was 7 years old, but I really only started to get into programming in July 2017 (13). I started to develop an interest in programming because of Cortana. I was fascinated by how she worked, all of her functionality, the way how all I had to do was say her name and she would respond, the way how she was able to recognise the words that I would say, and then even be able to ignore voices that were not my own. All of these factors and more led me to realize how cool and powerful Artificial Intelligence really could be. I was inspired to make my own to surpass her one day. And so, with some research, I discovered that Python is a very good language for Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence (AI). I began by learning Python 2 using Sololearn. It was a bit tedious, but I was having alot of fun. All I had to do was speak the same language, and I was able to have it do anything I wanted, create anything I wanted. It was like my own genie with unlimited wishes in a sense. I was the only one of all of the family members and people that I knew at the time that was into programming, so whenever I wanted help, I ended up happily either reading the documentations, or asking google a question.

My First Task

My programming in Python 2 had been just for me, just me learning and trying to improve, I never had to do anything for anyone, I was just trying to improve so I could eventually work for a big tech company and be able to help out alot of people. That was my idea back then. During October - November 2017, I had been asked me for my help in a project by my mother who was a student at the University Of The West Indies Cavehill Campus at the time. The part of the project that I was to help in was to create a mobile app using MIT App Inventor, to do a particular task. I had never dealt with MIT App Inventor before, but I was given a problem, and the developer stirring inside me wanted to solve it. Using youtube and google, I learnt how to use MIT App Inventor and was able to make the project in about a week. This was the first time that I had been able to use my technological skills for someone else, and it was alot of fun.

Web Development

After messing around with Python 2 and MIT App Inventor for a few months, I was met with 2 problems.

  1. The computer I was using no longer worked, so I had to use my mother's. But because it was her main one, my time on it was limited.
  2. School
Due to these 2 factors, my interest in the topic that I was teaching myself slowly vanished. On the 24 September 2018, I registered for a Computer Coding Camp held by the Caribbean Science Foundation, where I would spend the next 4 months learning about the web development basics (html, css, and Javascript). Back then I had developed a passion for Web Development, and put alot of my time into it to make sure that I understood everything. However, due to me having to share my mother's laptop, there were some classes that I could not attend, and others that I was not able to fully take advantage of. Despite that, I still was able to learn alot and make a decent project along with the members of my team. The reason why I was so vested in web development was because, like a fair amount of other things I do, I thought it was cool. Even before my interest in programming, the internet was my friend. I had been using different websites for so long, but not once had I really thought about how it was made, or why it works. To me, it just did. Now I was given the opportunity to learn the how and the why behind the websites that I had been using for years, and then was gifted the ability to make my own. It was honestly amazing. All I had to do was open my text editor of choice, type a few lines of markup language and I was on my way to creating a whole new project.

The Cooldown & Switch

I continued to use and attempt to improve at web development for about a year straight. However, my interest was more on learning Javascript, than it was on html and css. The reason was because my web design skills aren't all of that XD. So I decided that I would try to make up for it with functionality. Learning Javascript was a big thing for me. During the camp, we learnt alot of stuff, but by actually interacting with other programmers in a Discord Server I was in, I learnt far more efficient ways to do the same things that I was doing before. However, from around March 2019, I decided to take a break, and therefore stopped learning programming.

July 2019, I decided I wanted to get into game development. My mother no longer used her laptop as much, so I had alot more time on it and decided I would use it to pursue an interest. I, like alot of other children, love games. I would often play them for hours on end, on PC, PlaysStation2, NDS and even some Leap Frog products. I did some research and discovered that C++ was a brilliant language for game development. So I downloaded an Acrobat Document called "Guide to C++", and began to teach myself the language. I made a good few projects, and managed to stick with the language until February 2020.

Guide to C++ document used to teach myself the language.

During the period of November 2019 - February 2020, I started to lose interest in C++ because I couldn't figure out how to get graphics in. So my good few projects that I made were all CLI (Command Line Interface) driven. Because I wanted to actually see more than just words on the screen, I picked up web development once again and was constantly switching between the two. From Januray 2020, I had received my own laptop so developing was also a bit easier and I had alot more freedom as well.

Near the middle of February, I decided that I wanted to switch over to Linux - Ubuntu for a few reasons.

  1. I thought it was cool and developer like
  2. I thought it looked cool when I would install packages and watch all of the terminal code run down my screen
  3. I thought it was cool to use something different to everyone else that they hadn't even heard of before.
All in all, I thought it was cool XD. There I had gotten used to how weird the VIM text editor was, and it quickly became my favourite (Before, I had been using Notepad++ for web development, and Dev C++ for Cpp). Unfortunately, this didn't last long. I began to feel a bad about no longer being able to play alot of games with my friends, because the laptop I was using wasn't powerful enough to handle a windows virtual environment, (trust me I tried... alot). Then I began to get issues with Ubuntu itself, where it would just stop responding from time to time. I assumed that this was a problem with my device itself and, sadly, made the switch back to windows. Then I got an issue with my laptop. I wasn't sure if the charger had stopped working, or if there was something wrong with the port. So I stupidly tried another charger to test it and... it worked... then never again. So then I came to the conclusion that by using a different charger, I had fried the insides, and then that laptop never turned on again. So I was back using my mother's. This incident happened in late March. From here, I went on another break.

The Rise Of Python

2nd of June, 2020. With my mother's laptop once again, my interest in AI and Machine Learning was rekindled. A factor of this was CodeBullet, a youtuber who makes amazing A.I and inspired me to be interested in it again. I was ready to take on Python again, this time Python 3. With my mind set on being a Data Scientist once again, I began to put things in place. On this same day, I watched a 4 hour video from freecodecamp.org, and made several small projects to help me learn the language.

Projects made while learning python

On April 6th, I created my github account. On the 12th of June 2020, I started working on my first large project. A Discord Bot by the name of Isaiah's Parade. This was both very helpful and limiting at the same time. The python wrappy for discord, (discord.py) although easy to use, isn't really all that tailored for new python developers such as myself. So using it took asking alot of questions from the python and discord.py Discord servers that I was in, and alot of trial and error. As much knowledge that I was gaining while using this library, all of my knowledge was mainly about the library itself. Before I knew it, I had been swallowed in discord.py, unable to break out and learn the parts of python that I really wanted to learn. This had to change.

On the 17th of August, I received an email stating that I was invited to start learning on Coursera. I registered for it by the 19th of August, confirmed by account on 20th, and immediately enrolled into a 5 part course being offered by the University of Michigan, Python For Everybody, which I completed with an Honours on September 10th 2020.

Image of Certificate received on completing the course.

With this course completed, I felt as though I were equipped with all of the basic tools I needed to get started, and I began improving on my skills.

Robotics & Scratch

During the period of late July to Early August, I met with the mentor of the Barbados Robotics Team, Matthew Douglas. After telling him about my goal in being a Data Scientist, I was invited to become a member of the Team. Because of my experience, I was tasked with creating the website for the Team, and was soon a Team Leader. Even though Robotics in itself was not a passion of mine, I loved knowing that I would be able to code robots, and the amount of opportunities that would open before me. Soon we started to learn about the Programming Language of Scratch, which was to help introduce us to coding concepts. Because of my experience with programming, learning how to use Scratch was fairly simple, and I soon became someone that the other members of the teams could ask questions and receive help from for the language. However, Covid-19 was a huge dampener on everything, so everything was far slower, and it was impossible for us to travel and take part in competitions that year. Due to some big changes, the time I was able to put in the Barbados Robotics Team slowly dwindled down until my activity reached a standstill in January of 2021

Private Caribbean Study Goals

Shortly after becoming a member of the Barbados Robotics Team (BRT), I met with 16 year old Jasmine Greene, Founder of Private Caribbean Study Goals, an organisation created to help students from around the Caribbean that were doing CSEC & CAPE Exams, and a fellow member of the Barbados Robotics Team. After one of the Barbados Robotics Team meetings, I was invited by Ms.Greene, to one of her meetings that she was having at the time. Having heard of my "skills" from the BRT, I was asked if I could make a page for her organisation. After explaining that my design skills weren't all the best, and that I hadn't made a website in a long time (The Barbados Robotics Team website was made using Webflow.io), she continued to insist. The biggest problem at the time was that all of her large website is done using google sites, and I had no idea of how to integrate a page done in code into said site. Eventually, the idea was disregarded, and I no longer had to struggle with that field that I do not fancy. However, at this time, Ms.Greene had a discord server already up and running with over 1000 students, and was now in need of some discord bots to help her manage everything. This, is the field that I specialized in. I accepted her request and then created 1 moderator bot, and 3 music bots and 2 timer bots, in about 2 weeks, all made specifically for her server. As time continued and PCSG continued to grow, I began to feel even more inspired to get my own business up and running. On the 27th of February 2021, Ms.Greene and I formed a partnership between our 2 businesses.
